A sales lead becomes qualified when the prospect has a defined business problem, decision authority, budget visibility, and a target timeline.

Inbound leads must receive first response within 4 business hours.

Enterprise discovery calls must identify pain points, current process, decision makers, budget range, security requirements, and success criteria.

A lead should be marked high priority when expected annual contract value is above 25 lakh INR or the prospect has a go-live timeline under 60 days.

Pricing discounts up to 10 percent can be approved by the sales manager.

Pricing discounts above 10 percent and up to 20 percent require approval from the business head.

Pricing discounts above 20 percent require approval from the CEO and finance team.

Proposals must include scope, assumptions, exclusions, commercial terms, delivery timeline, support terms, and payment milestones.

No proposal should be sent before technical feasibility and delivery capacity are reviewed.

Sales handoff to delivery must include proposal, meeting notes, stakeholders, risks, commercial commitments, and success metrics.

Lost opportunities above 10 lakh INR must include a loss reason and competitor note in the CRM.
